How to Use This Kit

Zero Friction.
Maximum Reach.

This kit is designed so that any Arc member, board member, or volunteer can send outreach to a Texas special needs organization in under 5 minutes. No customization required — the copy is already written, the ask is already framed, and the recipient can share it with their community with one forward or copy-paste.

The ask is not for a donation. It is a community awareness partnership — "share this with your families." Zero cost to them. Massive reach for The Arc.

The 3-Step Outreach Process
1.Find the contact (use the Target Org list below)
2.Copy the email template that matches their org type
3.Paste it, add their name and org, hit send
Priority Outreach Targets

Who to
Reach First

These are the highest-leverage contacts in the Texas IDD ecosystem. One email to the right person in each category can reach hundreds of families.

DFW School Districts — SPED Departments
DISD, FWISD, PISD, Carroll ISD, HEB ISD, Grapevine-Colleyville ISD, Richardson ISD, Garland ISD, Arlington ISD
Contact the Special Education Director at each district. Ask them to share P4P with their parent communication channels and special education family networks. One email from a SPED director reaches hundreds of families.
Parent Support Organizations
Texas Parent to Parent · Down Syndrome Guild of Dallas · FEAT of North Texas · Autism Speaks DFW
These organizations run active parent listservs, Facebook groups, and email newsletters. A single share or forward from their leadership reaches their entire membership instantly.
Disability Advocacy & Service Organizations
United Cerebral Palsy Texas · Easterseals Texas · DARS (Disability and Rehab Services) · Navigate Life Texas · Special Olympics Texas
Peer organizations in the IDD ecosystem. Outreach should be framed as community partnership and awareness — not competition. "We want your families to know about this event" is the right framing.
Therapy Centers & Clinical Practices
ABA therapy centers, pediatric occupational therapy practices, speech therapy groups, developmental pediatricians across DFW
Clinicians see IDD families weekly. A flyer in the waiting room or a social media share from a therapy center's account reaches families at a high-trust moment.
Online Parent Communities
Facebook Groups: "DFW Special Needs Parents," "North Texas Autism Moms," "IDD Families Dallas Fort Worth," Nextdoor disability community boards
These groups are organic, high-trust, and actively looking for local events and resources. A post from an Arc member or admin share can generate significant ticket sales quickly.
