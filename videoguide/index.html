Practical Tips

How to Film It

Your phone shoots better video than most professional cameras from ten years ago. Use it. Here's how to make it look and sound great with zero equipment.

📱
Phone Settings — Check These First
  • Open your camera app and switch to video mode
  • If you have the option, shoot in 1080p — not 4K (file gets too large to send)
  • Make sure your storage isn't full before you start
  • Turn on Do Not Disturb so calls don't interrupt mid-take
☀️
Light — The Single Biggest Factor

Good lighting makes everything look better. It's the one thing that makes the biggest difference with the least effort.

✓ Do This
  • Face a window — let the light hit your face
  • Daytime natural light is ideal
  • Overcast days are actually great — soft and even
✗ Avoid This
  • Window behind you — you'll be a silhouette
  • Harsh overhead lighting that casts shadows
  • Dark rooms with a single lamp
🏠
Background — You Are the Focus
✓ Works Great
  • A plain wall — any color
  • A tidy, lived-in room
  • Outside in a shaded spot
  • Anywhere that feels like you
✗ Skip These
  • Cluttered or messy backgrounds
  • Busy public spaces with movement
  • Staged or corporate-looking spaces
🎙️
Sound — Almost As Important As Light

Bad audio loses viewers faster than anything. A few easy fixes make a huge difference.

✓ Do This
  • Film somewhere quiet — close windows, turn off fans and TVs
  • Get closer to the camera rather than raising your voice
  • Do a 10-second test and play it back with headphones
  • Use earbuds with a mic if you have them — clip the wire near your mouth
✗ Avoid This
  • Filming outside on a windy day
  • Rooms with a lot of echo
  • Near a busy road or HVAC unit
📷
Handheld or Propped Up — Both Work

Handheld feels natural and conversational. If you go handheld, hold with both hands and keep your elbows close to your body to reduce shake. Totally fine.

Propped up gives a cleaner, more stable shot. No tripod? Stack some books, use a mug, lean it against anything sturdy. Just make sure the camera is at eye level — not looking up from below or down from above. Eye level is everything.

🎯
How to Frame Yourself
  • Your face and chest should fill most of the frame — not your whole body head to toe
  • Leave a little space above your head — not too much
  • Look at the camera lens, not at yourself on the screen — it makes eye contact with the viewer
  • Center yourself or slightly to one side — both work great
🗣️
Pacing — Talk Like You're Talking to a Friend

The most common thing people get wrong on camera is speeding up. They rush, forget to breathe, and lose the warmth that made us want to film them in the first place.

Slow down. You will feel like you're talking too slowly. You are not. Natural conversation pace feels slow and lands perfectly for the viewer.

Pause. Pauses are powerful. If you need a moment to collect a thought, take it. A pause gives the viewer a moment to feel what you just said.

Don't restart every stumble. Say the thought again from the beginning of that sentence and keep going. We can cut the stumble in editing — a mid-thought restart is much easier to fix than you think.
