Audience Segmentation
Options Menu

Your mission serves three distinct audiences — but your list doesn't know that yet. Here are nine ways to change that, without being pushy, invasive, or asking too much of your team.

⬤  Donors & Corporate
⬤  IDD Families
⬤  Community

Prepared by JRZ Consulting Solutions  ·  For Arc of DFW Area Leadership Review

The Challenge

One List.
Three Completely Different People.

Right now, every email you send goes to the same list — corporate sponsors, IDD families, and individual donors all receiving the same message at the same time. That's not a content problem. It's a data problem.

The options below are not ranked. They are a menu. Some can be layered together. Some are one-time fixes. Some build long-term infrastructure. The right combination depends on what your team has capacity for — and that's the conversation we're here to have.

Nine Approaches

How to Start Knowing
Who's Who

Each option below includes what it is, how hard it is to execute, what platform it works with, and what data it actually produces.

01
Self-Selection Audience Quiz
The least pushy way to ask who someone is

Send a single email to your full list with one question: "Which best describes you?" — three clickable options (Donor / Family / Community Supporter). Each click routes to a different thank-you page and tags the contact in NeonOne or your email platform. No form. One click.

Effort
Low
Friction for Contact
Minimal — 1 click
Platform
NeonOne / Any email tool
Data Produced
Self-reported audience tag
How It Works

Write a warm, honest email explaining that you want to make sure you're sending the right messages to the right people. Three buttons. Each routes to a simple thank-you page. Tag on click. Done.

Best for: Getting a quick baseline read on your list composition. Expect 20–40% response rate from an engaged list. Even partial data is better than none.
02
Reply-Based Email Segmentation
Even lower tech — just reply with a number

Same concept as the quiz — but instead of clickable buttons, you ask contacts to reply with a number (1, 2, or 3). No links, no landing pages, no platform setup. Works in any email system. Responses get manually tagged in NeonOne.

Effort
Very Low
Friction for Contact
Minimal — reply to email
Platform
Any — no integration needed
Data Produced
Self-reported, manual tagging
How It Works

Send a warm, personal-feeling email. "Reply with 1 if you're a donor, 2 if you have a family member with IDD, 3 if you just care about the community." Someone monitors the inbox and tags each response in NeonOne. Takes half a day to process.

Best for: Organizations with limited technical setup. The reply format feels more personal and often gets higher response than a click — people feel like they're talking to a human.
03
Landing Page Lead Capture with Auto-Tagging
Segment at the point of entry — before they're even on your list

Build three separate opt-in pages — one for donors, one for IDD families, one for community supporters. Each page has its own form that automatically tags new contacts based on where they signed up. Anyone who finds you through the donor page is tagged as a donor from day one.

Effort
Medium
Friction for Contact
None — they choose their path
Platform
NeonOne forms / GitHub Pages
Data Produced
Automated tags at entry
How It Works

JRZ builds three audience-specific landing pages. Each has a unique URL and form. Sharing the right link in the right context (sponsor outreach → donor page, IDD Facebook groups → family page) builds a clean segmented list going forward. Does not fix the existing list — starts fresh from today.

Best for: Future-state infrastructure. Every new contact who joins from here forward will be properly tagged. Pairs well with Option 01 or 02 for the existing list.
04
Community Vulnerability Campaign
Tell them the problem and invite them to be part of the solution

Send an honest, open email to your full list: "We want to serve you better — but we need your help to do it." Explain that your current system doesn't allow you to distinguish between your different audiences, which means everyone gets the same message whether it's relevant to them or not. Vulnerability creates trust. Trust creates participation.

Effort
Low
Friction for Contact
Minimal — emotionally invited
Platform
Any email tool
Data Produced
Self-reported + community goodwill
How It Works

JRZ writes the email. It's warm, real, and direct — not a survey, not a form. It ends with a simple self-selection mechanism (Option 01 or 02 embedded). The vulnerability of the ask becomes the reason people respond. This approach consistently outperforms standard segmentation emails in mission-driven organizations.

Best for: Organizations with a warm, mission-connected list. The Arc's audience already cares — this gives them a reason to lean in. The honesty differentiates you from every other org in their inbox.
05
One-Time List Enrichment
Research your existing contacts to fill in what you don't know

Take your current list and run it through a manual research pass — using names, email addresses, and public information to identify whether a contact is a business (corporate donor), an individual, or an IDD-adjacent family member. A one-time sprint that gets your existing list up to date with no action required from the contacts themselves.

Effort
High — one-time sprint
Friction for Contact
Zero — they never know
Platform
LinkedIn / Google / NeonOne
Data Produced
Inferred profile data
How It Works

Export your list. For each contact with a real name and email, run a quick LinkedIn or Google search. Business email domains categorize immediately. Personal emails can often be cross-referenced. Contacts with obvious IDD family connection (prior event registrations, conference attendees) get tagged. Unknowns route to Option 01 or 02.

Best for: Getting a meaningful head start before any campaign goes live. Even tagging 30–40% of your list transforms your ability to send targeted content. Best executed by a dedicated volunteer or intern over 1–2 days.
06
In-Person Networking & Live CRM Updating
Tag people at the moment you meet them

At every Arc event, board meeting, conference, and community gathering — the person at check-in tags each contact in NeonOne in real time. "Are you here as a donor, a family member, or a community supporter?" is a natural question at a registration table. The answer goes straight into the CRM.

Effort
Ongoing — low per event
Friction for Contact
None — natural conversation
Platform
NeonOne — mobile access
Data Produced
High-confidence, in-person verified
How It Works

Designate one volunteer per event whose job includes CRM tagging. Build a simple NeonOne lookup into the check-in flow. P4P alone will give you 150–200 verified audience tags in one evening. A Look Ahead Conference attendees are almost entirely IDD families — tag everyone who checks in.

Best for: Building the highest-confidence data over time. In-person tags are more reliable than any self-report mechanism. P4P on September 27 is a natural first deployment point.
07
Social Content Self-Selection
Watch who responds to what — let behavior do the segmenting

Post three distinct pieces of content in the same week — each targeted at a different audience (sponsor-focused, family-resource, donor story). Track who engages with which posts. The people who comment on the family resource post are likely IDD families. The people who share the sponsor post are likely business-connected. Soft data, but real signal.

Effort
Low — content you're posting anyway
Friction for Contact
Zero — passive observation
Platform
Facebook / LinkedIn
Data Produced
Behavioral signal — soft tag
How It Works

No extra work required — these are posts you'd be making during the campaign anyway. The insight comes from reviewing engagement manually or through Facebook Insights. Not precise enough to build a full segmentation strategy on, but useful for validating other approaches and spotting surprises in who's paying attention.

Best for: Supplementing other methods with behavioral confirmation. Use alongside Option 01 or 04 to cross-reference self-reported data with actual behavior.
08
Referral Pathway Tagging
Tag people by where they came from — not who they say they are

Create unique registration and signup links for each audience channel. The link shared in an IDD Facebook group is different from the link shared in a corporate newsletter. When someone signs up through either link, they're automatically tagged by source — no extra question required. The channel they came through tells you who they are.

Effort
Medium — setup required
Friction for Contact
Zero — invisible to them
Platform
NeonOne UTM / URL params
Data Produced
Inferred tag by source channel
How It Works

JRZ creates tagged URLs for each campaign channel. When NeonOne captures the registration, the source tag comes with it. Requires setup but runs automatically from that point forward. Pairs naturally with the landing page approach in Option 03 and P4P's multi-channel distribution.

Best for: Passive, scalable segmentation. Once set up, every new sign-up through any channel is automatically contextualized. Zero friction for the contact, zero ongoing effort for the team.
09
Event Check-In Intake Question
One question at registration captures what you need

Add a single optional question to your P4P event registration form and the A Look Ahead Conference registration: "What best describes your relationship to The Arc?" Three options. One click. Every person who registers for any Arc event from this point forward self-identifies at the point of registration — and that answer flows directly into NeonOne.

Effort
Low — one form field
Friction for Contact
Minimal — optional field
Platform
NeonOne registration forms
Data Produced
Self-reported at highest intent moment
How It Works

Add the question to the NeonOne event registration form for P4P 2026 before tickets go live. Every ticket buyer answers it. The answer tags them automatically. P4P alone could tag 100–200 contacts in a single registration window — your warmest, most engaged audience, all identified at the moment they're most excited about The Arc.

Best for: Capturing your highest-intent contacts with the least friction. People registering for an event are already engaged — they're happy to tell you who they are. Easiest high-confidence data you'll ever collect.
JRZ Recommended Starting Point

You Don't Need All Nine.
You Need the Right Three.

A Practical Stack for
Before August 3

Every option above is valid. But trying to run all nine at once is how nothing gets done. Here's the combination that gets you meaningful segmentation data before the campaign goes live — without overwhelming your team.

Step 1
Option 04 + 02 — Community Vulnerability Campaign with Reply Segmentation. Send one honest email to your full list. Ask them to reply with a number. Manual tagging takes half a day. Gets you a baseline read on your existing contacts before the campaign launches.
Step 2
Option 09 — Event Check-In Intake on P4P Registration. Add one question to the ticket form before it goes live. Every new registrant is tagged automatically. Zero ongoing effort after setup.
Step 3
Option 03 — Landing Page Lead Captures for future contacts. JRZ builds audience-specific entry points. Everyone who joins your list after August 3 is tagged from day one. Builds infrastructure for 2027 and beyond.
