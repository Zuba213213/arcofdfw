Who They Are

Over 50 Years of
Advocacy Without Apology

Founded in 1971, The Arc of DFW Area has grown from a single Birdville town hall to the leading all-volunteer advocacy organization for individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities across the entire DFW Metroplex. They don't provide direct services — they do something harder. They change the systems, the laws, and the culture that shape every life with IDD.

"The Arc of DFW Area empowers individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities and their families to live life as they define it through advocacy, awareness, training, and support."

The Marketing Insight

Two Audiences.
One Powerful Story.

The Arc runs two parallel conversations simultaneously — one with the people who fund the mission, and one with the people the mission exists to serve. These audiences have completely different motivations, language, and conversion triggers. This vault is built to honor both without confusing them.

Audience 01 — Donors & Corporate Partners

They're buying belonging, visibility, and purpose.

Corporate sponsors want ESG alignment, employee engagement, and brand association with a cause their community respects. Individual donors want to feel like their giving matters — they want to see the face behind the mission, not just a tax receipt. Both audiences respond to story, social proof, and the sense that they are part of something lasting.

Audience 02 — Individuals with IDD & Families

They're navigating a system that was not built for them.

Families with IDD members are often overwhelmed, undertrained, and underresourced. They need a navigator — someone who speaks their language, knows the Medicaid waitlists, the IEP process, the housing gaps. The Arc's communication to this audience must be warm, clear, and empowering — never clinical, never condescending.
