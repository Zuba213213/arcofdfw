Section 01

Voice & Tone

The Arc of DFW Area speaks from a position of earned authority and deep human care. This is not a brand that shouts. It doesn't need to. Fifty-five years of showing up for families no one else was fighting for gives this organization a quiet, powerful credibility — and every piece of copy should honor that.

The voice is different depending on which audience you're speaking to, but the core character never changes: warm, clear, purposeful, and deeply human.

Warm — Not Sentimental

The Arc's tone is filled with genuine care for the people it serves. But it doesn't wallow in pity or use disability as a prop. The families and individuals The Arc serves are resilient, capable, and deserving of dignity — the copy reflects that. Emotion earns its place through specificity, not manipulation.

Clear — Not Clinical

The systems surrounding IDD are complex. The Arc's language is not. Whether explaining a Medicaid waitlist or a sponsorship package, write in plain language that a parent, a donor, or a CEO can absorb quickly. Jargon is only used when it serves the reader — and always explained when it does.

Purposeful — Not Pushy

Every communication has a clear reason to exist. Every CTA asks for one thing. The Arc does not use scarcity tactics, guilt, or high-pressure language. It leads with value, shares proof, and trusts the reader to respond. Because they will, when the story is told well.

Credible — Not Boastful

55 years. 100% volunteer-run. Real families, real outcomes, real advocacy wins. The Arc's credibility lives in its track record, not its marketing language. Let the facts do the work. Lead with them, state them simply, and let readers draw their own conclusions about the organization's integrity.

Dual-Tuned: Donor vs. Family

Donor copy is confident, community-oriented, and impact-focused — it speaks to belonging, visibility, and legacy. Family and client copy is warm, practical, and empowering — it speaks to navigation, knowledge, and not being alone. These two tones should never be mixed in the same piece of communication.

Section 02

Color Palette

The Arc's official brand colors are gold and navy. The marketing vault expands this into a working palette that assigns specific emotional jobs to each color — ensuring consistency across all materials.

Deep Navy
#0D1B2A
Authority, trust, professionalism. Backgrounds, headers.
Arc Gold
#C9A84C
Warmth, legacy, impact. Primary accent, donor communications, CTAs.
Mission Coral
#C95B38
Humanity, urgency, heart. IDD family communications, individual donor stories.
Advocacy Teal
#2A7B6F
Empowerment, growth, community. Self-Determination Group, advocacy programs.
Warm Ivory
#F5F0E8
Approachability, readability. Body text on dark, light-mode section backgrounds.

Color Assignment Rule: Gold = Donor/Corporate communications. Coral = Individual donor and human-story content. Teal = IDD families and client-facing programs. Navy = Always the foundation. Never use gold and coral together in the same headline — they represent different audiences and should stay in their lanes.

Section 03

Typography

Two typefaces. Used consistently, they give every Arc communication a recognizable, elevated identity without ever looking templated.

Display / Headlines — Playfair Display
Every Life, Self-Determined.
Playfair Display · Serif · Weight 700 for headlines, 400 italic for pull quotes · Google Fonts

Use for: All major headlines, event titles, quote callouts, document titles. Creates gravitas and warmth simultaneously — reads like a publication that has been around a long time.

Body / UI — Lato
The Arc of DFW Area has been advocating for individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities since 1971. We are 100% volunteer-run. Every dollar raised goes directly to programs, advocacy, and the families we serve.
Lato · Sans-serif · Weight 400 for body, 700 for labels/eyebrows · Google Fonts

Use for: All body text, navigation, captions, data labels, email body. Clean and highly legible across all ages and screen sizes.

Minimum size rule: No body text below 16px in print or digital. Aim for 18px on screens. Many of The Arc's audience members are older adults or individuals with visual processing differences — readability is an accessibility issue, not just an aesthetic one.

Section 04

On-Brand vs.
Off-Brand

The fastest way to internalize The Arc's voice is to see what it is — and what it isn't. These examples cover email, social, and event copy.

Talking About the People We Serve

On-Brand"Maria's son has the right to a self-determined life. The Arc exists to make sure the systems around him make that possible."
Off-Brand"These disabled individuals need our help. Without donors like you, they would have no hope."
On-Brand"Individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities (IDD)"
Off-Brand"the disabled," "special needs," "suffers from," "afflicted with"

Donor Ask Language

On-Brand"Your support makes the next family's first phone call a little less lonely. Give today."
Off-Brand"DONATE NOW before it's too late! These families are counting on YOU!!!"
On-Brand"We've been at this for 55 years. We'll be here for 55 more. Your gift continues that work."
Off-Brand"We desperately need your help to keep our organization alive."

Corporate Partner Language

On-Brand"Sponsoring The Arc of DFW is one of the most credible community investments a DFW business can make. Here's exactly what you receive at each tier."
Off-Brand"Please consider donating to our cause. Any amount helps and we really appreciate your generosity."

Event Promotion

On-Brand"Putts for Progress. Mini golf. Real change. September 2026 at Puttshack Addison. $50 gets you in. Every dollar stays in DFW."
Off-Brand"Come join us for our annual fundraising gala in support of our mission to empower individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities across the greater Dallas-Fort Worth metropolitan area."
