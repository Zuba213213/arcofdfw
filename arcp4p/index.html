Section 03

Grassroots &
Low-Cost Tactics

Eight high-impact tactics that cost almost nothing except time. Collectively these can add 40–80 attendees and $5,000–$10,000 in revenue the paid strategy cannot reach.

🏫
TX Special Needs Program Outreach
Cost: $0 · Impact: High reach
Build a master contact list of DFW special education parent groups, therapy centers, IEP advocacy networks, and disability orgs across Texas. Send the Outreach Kit to every contact — flyer + copy-paste social post + email template. One email to the right SPED director reaches hundreds of families. Priority targets: Down Syndrome Guild of Dallas, FEAT of North Texas, Autism Speaks DFW, Texas Parent to Parent, Navigate Life Texas, United Cerebral Palsy Texas, and all major DFW school district SPED departments.
🤝
Ambassador Invite Program
Cost: $0 · Impact: 30–60 additional attendees
Identify 10–15 Arc members, board members, and advocates. Give each a personal invite script, shareable digital assets, and a commitment to invite 5 people. A personal invite converts 3–5x better than mass email. Brief them in a 30-minute meeting. Check in at the 4-week mark on confirmed guest count.
🏆
Inter-Company Tournament Competition
Cost: $0 · Impact: Turns $225 into $900+
Reframe the tournament as a corporate bragging rights competition. Create a named trophy. Market it on LinkedIn: "Which DFW company brings the best team this year?" Companies who sponsor get reserved team slots. The competitive angle gives HR directors a reason to bring 8–12 employees instead of 4. Post the leaderboard live at the event and on social after.
📰
Local Media Outreach
Cost: $0 · Impact: Audience multiplier
Send a press release 6 weeks out to: Dallas Morning News community section, CultureMap Dallas, Do214, Addison Magazine, WFAA Good Morning Texas, NBC5 Community Calendar, CBS11 local features. The hook: "55-year-old all-volunteer nonprofit hosts annual mini golf fundraiser that raised $34,000 in a single evening — and is aiming higher this year."
⛳
Puttshack Co-Brand Ask
Cost: $0 · Impact: New audience
Puttshack Addison has an active social following and benefits from showcasing their venue for a cause. Ask their marketing team for one co-branded social post, inclusion in their event-booker email, and a venue newsletter mention. Frame it as mutual visibility — they look like a community partner, The Arc reaches new audiences.
📱
Volunteer Social Takeover Week
Cost: $0 · Impact: Authentic FOMO content
The week before the event, 5 volunteers take over The Arc's Facebook story for one day each. Each shares why they're coming and what The Arc means to them. No production needed — raw authenticity converts better than polished content in this community. Brief: "60 seconds. Your face. Your reason. Tell people to come."
⏰
Early Bird Prize Drawing
Cost: Donated prize · Impact: Front-loads revenue
Tickets purchased 6+ weeks out enter a prize drawing — dinner for two, a Puttshack round for four, or a donated business gift. This converts fence-sitters early, gives Arc real attendance numbers for sponsor pitches, and reduces last-minute scramble. Solicit the prize from a sponsor so net cost is zero.
📅
North Texas Giving Day Alignment
Cost: $0 · Impact: $4,000–$6,000 additional
North Texas Giving Day falls in September — same month as P4P. Run a unified narrative: "This September, two ways to support the IDD community in DFW." P4P attendees become NTXGD donors. NTXGD donors become P4P ticket buyers. One campaign, one month, one community.
Section 04

Story Video
Scripts

Four 60–90 second videos, shot on a phone, deployed weekly for 4 weeks before the event. No production budget needed. Each follows the same arc: identity → struggle → The Arc → invitation.

Video 1 — The Parent

Week 6 · Facebook · Awareness

Shot on phone, speaker looks directly at camera. Natural home or outdoor setting. No script in hand — bullet points memorized or prompted off-screen.

0:00–0:15
[Natural self-introduction]"My name is [Name]. My [son/daughter] [Name] has [diagnosis]. They are [age] years old and one of the most [genuine trait] people I know."
0:15–0:45
[The struggle — be specific, not dramatic]"When [he/she] was [age], we were trying to figure out [IEP / Medicaid / housing / transition]. I didn't know where to start. I didn't know who to call. I didn't even know the right questions to ask. We were doing this completely alone."
0:45–1:10
[The Arc's role — specific]"Then we found The Arc of DFW. What changed wasn't that someone fixed everything. What changed was that we weren't alone anymore. We had information. We had community. We had people who understood."
1:10–1:25
[Direct invitation — look into camera]"This September, The Arc is hosting Putts for Progress at Puttshack in Addison. $50 gets you in. I'll be there. I hope you'll come."
End card
[Text overlay]PUTTS FOR PROGRESS 2026 · [DATE] · PUTTSHACK ADDISON · LINK IN BIO

Video 2 — The Self-Advocate

Week 5 · Facebook · Community

Individual with IDD speaks for themselves — most powerful video in the series. Keep it fully in their own voice. Let silences happen. Arc staff present for support but not directing. Authenticity is the entire asset.

0:00–0:20
"My name is [Name]. I am [age] years old. I have [diagnosis/identity]. And I am a member of The Self-Determination Group at The Arc of DFW."
0:20–0:50
[What The Arc means to them]"Before I found this group, I [felt isolated / didn't have people who understood / didn't know I had rights]. Now I [specific change]. The Arc taught me that I get to decide what my life looks like. Nobody else gets to decide that for me."
0:50–1:10
"There is an event this September called Putts for Progress. It helps The Arc keep doing what it does. I'm going to be there. It would mean a lot if you came too. It's mini golf and it's actually really fun."
End card
[Text overlay]PUTTS FOR PROGRESS 2026 · [DATE] · LINK IN BIO

Video 3 — The Urgency Video

Week 4 · Facebook + LinkedIn · Push

Board member, volunteer, or Arc staff. More direct and campaign-focused. Can be shot at Puttshack during a preview visit for visual context. Tone: energized and warm, not salesy.

0:00–0:15
"We are [X] weeks away from Putts for Progress 2026. And I want to tell you exactly what's at stake."
0:15–0:40
"Last year this event raised $34,000. In one evening. For families across DFW navigating one of the hardest things a family can face. This year we want to do more. And we're close. But we need you in the room."
0:40–1:00
"Tickets are $50. Tournament is $75. Bring your team for $225. Great night at Puttshack in Addison — every dollar goes to advocacy and programs for the IDD community right here in DFW. Link in bio. Let's go."
End card
[Ticket link prominently displayed]TICKETS AVAILABLE NOW · LINK IN BIO

Video 4 — The Final Call

Week 2 · All Channels · Last Push

Short, punchy, visual. Rapid montage of previous video faces plus event visuals with voiceover or on-screen text. Under 60 seconds. Most direct of the four.

0:00–0:10
[Montage — 3-second clips from Videos 1–3]On-screen text: "These are the people you're showing up for."
0:10–0:30
[Speaker or voiceover]"Putts for Progress is [X] days away. We have [X] tournament spots left. General admission still available. $50. Puttshack Addison. [DATE]."
0:30–0:50
"The Arc of DFW has been fighting for families like the ones you just saw for 55 years. This is one night a year where you get to stand in that room with them. We hope to see you there."
End card

Section 06

Day-Of
Messaging Guide

What gets said in the room — from the stage, at check-in, and in the 48 hours surrounding the event — determines whether a first-time attendee becomes a recurring donor.

📋
Check-In Table Script
"Welcome to Putts for Progress! You're checked in. Here's your wristband and scorecard if you're in the tournament. Bar is open, food is on the [left/right]. Tournament starts at [time]. If you want to know more about The Arc while you're here, grab a card from the table — and genuinely, thank you for being here tonight."
🎤
Opening Remarks — 3 Minutes
"Good evening. My name is [Name], [role] at The Arc of DFW Area. We've advocated for individuals with intellectual and developmental disabilities in this community for 55 years. We are 100% volunteer-run. Every dollar raised tonight stays in DFW.

Last year, this room raised $34,000. Tonight, we want to do better — not because we set arbitrary goals, but because there are families in this city who needed The Arc last year, and there will be more next year, and the work doesn't stop.

So thank you for being here. Play well. Give generously. Enjoy the night."
📣
Mid-Event Fundraising Update
"We're halfway through the evening. As of right now, between ticket sales, sponsorships, and giving tonight, we have raised $[X]. Our goal is $[Y]. If you haven't given yet — QR code on every table. Thirty seconds. It matters."
🏁
Closing Remarks + Total Reveal
"Before we announce tournament results — one number. Tonight, this room raised $[FINAL TOTAL] for the IDD community in DFW. [Pause. Let it land.] That number represents families who will get the phone call they needed. Training they couldn't afford. Advocacy that changes laws.

Tournament results: [announce winners]. Thank you to our sponsors: [list by name]. And thank you to every single person in this room. We'll see you next year."
